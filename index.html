Certificate of Incorporation

The Certificate of Incorporation is the official foundation document for private limited liability companies, incorporated companies, and joint stock companies. It is issued by the registration authorities of the jurisdiction where such a company is legally incorporated. An important feature is that all legal regulations and financial requirements make the company comply with the laws of the country that issued such Certificate of Incorporation.

Certificate of Good Standing

Certificate of Good Standing is a document issued to official companies by the registration authorities in some countries. The document confirms the company's legal status and the absence of any arrears in payment of taxes, government fees, and duties. Such a certificate can only be issued if the company's status is "proper" and the company complies with all requirements and legal regulations of its jurisdiction.

Certificate of Notary

It is a document certifying the authenticity of anything: signatures, documents, purchase agreements, translation of documents, and other notarial acts. Such a certificate can be required by official authorities of the Company's registrar and in foreign jurisdictions. Notarized copies of the Company's constitutional and/or incorporation documents shall properly replace the originals of such documents kept at the administrative office.

Apostille

It is a special stamp affixed to official documents for their legalization as required by the 1961 Hague Convention. The Convention defines a strict list of persons entitled to sign for apostille. These are usually judges, public notaries, and registry clerks. Some countries have introduced a separate position - certifying officer. He or she is an official who certifies documents or copies thereof. The signature of such an official shall be certified later by a standard apostille.

Certificate of Tax Exemption

A certificate of tax exemption or certificate of tax residency is a legally established exemption from payment of any taxes for a certain category of taxpayers. Such a certificate confirms the company's tax status in the country of its official registration. A tax residency certificate issued by the tax registrar allows the company to exclude double taxation in some operations. For example, it is needed when buying real estate or conducting activities outside the country of official registration, opening a foreign representative office (for tax registration), or opening an account in a foreign bank.
